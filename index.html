📖 About This Project

The Story

The Ethiopian Peace Archive preserves and amplifies the voices of everyday Ethiopians and global supporters who believe in peace, unity, and coexistence. Over 1,000 voices were collected across all Ethiopian regions—Tigray, Amhara, Oromia, Somali, Afar, Sidama, SNNPR, and beyond—alongside messages of solidarity from around the world.

These voices were gathered over five years (2017-2022), spanning pre-conflict hope, wartime resilience, and post-conflict reconciliation. Each voice represents a real person who chose to speak for peace when division was louder.

Methodology

Voices were collected through in-person interviews, written submissions, and community gatherings across Ethiopia. All contributors gave consent for their anonymized words to be shared publicly. Names and identifying details are kept confidential to protect contributors. Each quote has been translated into English while preserving the original language for authenticity.

Why This Matters

Because the world needs to hear what Ethiopians actually want—not just what conflict narratives say. Because Ethiopian voices deserve to be preserved, amplified, and honored. Because peace is built on understanding, and understanding starts with listening.
